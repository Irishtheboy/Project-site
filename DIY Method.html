2. How to make a Geometric Mouse Pad

Materials: Scrap of leather;old mouse pad, spray adhesive, rotary cutter, long ruler or quilter’s ruler, chalk, self healing mat

1. Spray a generous layer of adhesive to the top of the mouse pad and lay the leather on top. Take care to press and smooth the leather into place.

2. Using a ruler and chalk, mark out how you want to cut your mouse pad.

3. Following your chalk guidelines, cut your mouse pad out, using a rotary cutter, ruler, and self healing mat. You may need to pass over each cut several times to slice all the way through.

And your work should be done! So Simple.

3. How to make a Leather Hair Bow

Materials: Faux Leather – Found ours at Hobby Lobby in the Home Decorating Fabrics, Leather (optional) – Found this pretty aqua leather at Michaels Craft Store, Hair Clips, Glue Gun, Scissors

1. Cut out your 3 leather pieces. Your first piece will be 7″ x 1 1/4″, your second piece will be 4″ x 1 1/4″ and your third piece will be 1/2″ x 2 1/2″.

2. Glue the ends of the largest leather piece together.

3. Glue the two centers together.

4. Pinch the center together and glue.

5. Take your 2nd piece of leather and notch the ends. Then, center your bow on top and glue to the “pinched” part of the bow.

6. Take the smallest leather piece and wrap around the center of the bow and glue on the back.

7. Decide what you want to attach the bow to. You can decide on what clip to use. Once you've decided, you can cover your clip with some ribbon and hot glue. Then, hot glue the bow to the clip.

And your work should be done! So Elegant.

5. How to make a Leather Cushion

Materials: Leather, Fabric Materials, Fabric Scissors, Sewing Machine, Zipper, Color Threads, Pins.

1. The covers we made have leather for one side and canvas fabric for the other. Place the right sides of your leather and fabric piece together and mark the placement of your zipper. The zipper should be slightly shorter than the edge of your cover

2. Sew the edges that aren't covered by the zipper, then open up the seams.

3. Pin your zipper down the opening, but only on the seam allowance, then sew.

4. This is what your zipper should look like on the right side after sewing. We used a copper toothed zipper for this design, but feel free to use an invisible zipper if that's what you prefer.

5. Open up your zipper, then sew around the 3 remaining sides of your cushion cover. And simple as that, your custom cushion cover is finished!
